The Norwegian Nobel Committee has awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for 2006, to Muhammad Yunus and the Grameen Bank of Bangladesh. Professor Yunus has pioneered the use of micro-credit to lift very poor people out of poverty. He founded the Grameen Bank in 1976.

In making the award, the Nobel Committee said, "Lasting peace cannot be achieved unless large population groups find ways in which to break out of poverty. Micro-credit is one such means. Development from below also serves to advance democracy and human rights."
