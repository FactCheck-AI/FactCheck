THE best player to hold a British passport was recognised for his outstanding feats late last night when Steve Nash capped a remarkable season with the Phoenix Suns by being named the NBA’s most valuable player.

The MVP award is seen as fitting reward for the 31-year-old point guard’s achievements in helping to turn a team who won only 29 regular-season games a year ago into one whose 62-20 win-loss record was the best in the league.

Nash is the only significant addition to last season’s Suns, having made the move to Arizona after the Dallas Mavericks, his former club, refused to match the $65.6 million (£34.7 million), six-year contract on offer from Phoenix.
