Adams National Historical Park, located in Quincy, Massachusetts, is a significant site that preserves the home of two U.S. presidents, John Adams and John Quincy Adams. It also served as the residence of U.S. envoy Charles Francis Adams, and notable writers and historians Henry Adams and Brooks Adams. The park provides a unique opportunity to explore the lives and contributions of these influential figures.

Main Historic Features of the Park

The main historic features of Adams National Historical Park include the birthplaces of John Adams and John Quincy Adams, and the Stone Library. The library houses more than 14,000 historic volumes in 12 languages, offering a rich resource for those interested in history and literature. These sites provide a deep insight into the lives and works of the Adams family.

Guided Tours at the Park

Adams National Historical Park offers regularly scheduled guided tours of the houses during the season, which runs from April 19 to November 10. These tours provide an in-depth look at the historic homes and the lives of the Adams family who lived there.
