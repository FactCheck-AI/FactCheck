John Archibald Macdonald was an advertising writer, newspaper reporter and editor. He lived at 1055 Denman Street in 1901 and at 1856 Barclay Street in 1903.

Family Background

John Archibald Macdonald was born in Glasgow, Scotland, on September 12, 1877. (He was often called “Jack.”) His parents were Archibald MacDonald (1851–1921) and Helen Campbell Fletcher (1851–1940).

The Macdonald family moved from Scotland to Winnipeg, Manitoba in 1883. Archibald Macdonald established a successful contracting business. He died in Winnipeg on October 8, 1921.

Archibald’s wife, Helen, later moved to British Columbia. She died in Kelowna on August 4, 1940.

John’s Life and Career

John lived in Winnipeg with his parents until about 1900.

On January 15, 1900, in Winnipeg, he married Ida Eleanor Heath Toms. She was born in Wingham, Ontario, on January 26, 1878.

In 1901, John and Ida were living in Vancouver. John was an advertising writer, working from number 35 in the Flack Block in Vancouver. According to the Vancouver directory, the occupant of that office was the Standard Life Insurance Company.

By 1903, John was an advertising manager with the Vancouver News-Advertiser.

From 1901 to 1909, John appeared at the following addresses in Vancouver.

1901 1055 Denman Street 1902 Not listed under Vancouver names 1903 1856 Barclay Street 1904 to 1905 Not listed under Vancouver names 1906 1772 Davie Street (rear) 1907 to 1909 1787 Burnaby Street (1789 Burnaby Street in 1909 Vancouver names) 1910 Not listed under Vancouver names

In 1911, the Macdonalds were in Winnipeg, where John was a reporter for the Winnipeg Free Press.

During the First World War, John was in Ottawa, working as a fuel controller for the federal government. He later returned to his job as a reporter for the Winnipeg Free Press.

In 1921, John was the publicity commissioner for the province of Manitoba.

In 1924, the Macdonalds returned to Vancouver. John joined the staff of the Vancouver Sun, where he remained until he retired in 1948.

He died in Vancouver on November 18, 1951.

Ida died in Winnipeg on February 24, 1957.

John and Ida had the following children.

Archibald Argyle Mcdonald

Archibald Argyle Macdonald was born in Vancouver on November 22, 1900. (He was sometimes called “Archibald A. Macdonald” or “Archie A. Macdonald.”)

From 1901 to 1921 he was living with his parents.

In 1951 he was the assistant city editor on the New York News.

Nora Louise MacDonald

Nora Louise MacDonald was born in Winnipeg, Manitoba, on January 24, 1902.

In 1911 and 1921 she was living with her parents.

On June 27, 1930, in Winnipeg, she married Robert Swan Robertson.

Norah died in Winnipeg on October 2, 1975.

Robert died in Winnipeg on November 19, 1985.

Donald Boyd MacDonald

Donald Boyd MacDonald was born in Vancouver on April 28, 1905.

In 1911 and 1921 he was living with his parents.

He later worked as a grain clerk and wholesale credit manager.

On June 29, 1929, in Vancouver, he married Olive Ruthven Foster. She was born in Vancouver on April 13, 1907. Her parents were William Washborough Foster and Olive Stuart.

Donald died in Vancouver on December 31, 1973.

Olive died in Vancouver on October 18, 1999.

Helen Marion MacDonald

Helen Marion MacDonald was born on June 28, 1909, probably in Vancouver (although some sources say that she was born in Winnipeg, Manitoba).

In 1951 she was “Mrs. D.L. Ross” of Montreal.

She later married Charles Saxton Humphrey, who was a chemical engineer. He was born in Hornell, New York, on September 19, 1903. Charles died in Victoria, British Columbia, on August 29, 1984.

Helen died in Victoria on January 29, 1986.
