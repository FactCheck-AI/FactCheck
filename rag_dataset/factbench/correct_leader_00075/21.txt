FINLAND’S Prime Minister resigned yesterday amid accusations that she lied in parliament about the leaking of government documents about the Iraq war to win office.

Anneli Jaatteenmaki, the country’s first woman Prime Minister, denied lying, but failed to convince the public and sceptical politicians.

“If trust goes, it goes,” the 48-year-old conservative said, announcing her resignation.

Mrs Jaatteenmaki, from the Centre Party, managed to topple the incumbent Prime Minister, Paavo Lipponen, three months ago by hinting that his Government was compromising Finland’s proudly held neutrality by secretly backing George Bush on Iraq.

The majority of Finns were against the war and President Bush was deeply unpopular.
