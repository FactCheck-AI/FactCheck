Birthplace of William Gladstone, Liverpool, 19th century. The house in Rodney Street in which Gladstone was born. William Ewart Gladstone MP (1809-1898), British Liberal Prime Minister, was born in Liverpool and educated at Oxford from where in 1832 he embarked on his political career. He became Prime Minister in 1868, and then a further two times in 1880 and 1886. Whilst in power Gladstone brought in major reforms to Ireland and established a system of national education in England

EDITORS COMMENTS

This print captures the birthplace of one of Britain's most influential figures, William Ewart Gladstone. The image showcases the house in Rodney Street, Liverpool, where Gladstone was born in the 19th century. As a renowned British Liberal Prime Minister, Gladstone left an indelible mark on history with his political career that spanned several decades. Educated at Oxford University, Gladstone embarked on his political journey in 1832 and went on to become Prime Minister three times: first in 1868, then again in 1880 and 1886. During his time in power, he introduced significant reforms to Ireland and established a national education system in England. The Victorian-era building depicted in this engraving stands as a testament to the humble beginnings from which such greatness emerged. Its exterior exudes an air of elegance and historical significance that transports us back to a bygone era. As we gaze upon this monochrome masterpiece created by Butterworth & Heath (not the company), we are reminded of the immense impact individuals can have on society through their dedication and leadership. This print serves as both a visual delight for art enthusiasts and a poignant reminder of William Ewart Gladstone's enduring legacy.
