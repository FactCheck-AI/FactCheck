In 1907, Rudyard Kipling was awarded the Nobel Laureate in Literature "in consideration of the power of observation, originality of imagination, virility of ideas and remarkable talent for narration, which characterize the creations of this world-famous author".

Among Kipling's best known works are The Jungle Book and How the Leopard Got His Spots.
