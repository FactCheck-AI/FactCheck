Cast: Doug Schutte (Frank King), Corey Music (Carter King), Tony Smith (Clinton King), Abby Braune (Carol King), Joey Eberling (Kennedy King), Megan Adair (Wendy Reina), Doug Schutte (Stein King)

Notes: The Bard’s Town Theatre is a bar and restaurant which also serves as a small theatre for local plays. Due to increases in rent, the establishment has announced that it will most likely either close or move to a new location after this season of shows. The Kings of Christmas specifically is a tradition which the Bard’s Theatre has been performing annually since 2011. While staying a niche production, over the years it has worked up a cult following in Louisville, with local arts media outlet LEO Weekly describing it as “one of the the funniest shows you’ll see this holiday season,” “a must-see,” “flat-out brilliant,” and “comic writing of a high order.” Given that this is probably the last year in which the show can be seen in its original venue, I feel that I have captured something of local historical significance and would love to share it with you all. It’s very silly but quite amusing in my humble opinion. (Definitely NSFW in places though!)
