Intro and history: Netscape Communications Corporation was an American computer services company best known for Netscape Navigator, its web browser. It was founded in 1994 by Jim Clark and Marc Andreessen. The company played a pivotal role in the development of the commercial web in the mid-1990s before its acquisition by AOL in 1999. Industry: Computer Software Product, Services and Operations: Netscape's primary product was the web browser, Netscape Navigator. The company also developed server software, including the Netscape Commerce Server and Netscape Communications Server. Funding Details: Netscape went public in 1995, and its initial public offering (IPO) is considered one of the most successful in history. The company was acquired by AOL in a deal worth $4.2 billion in 1999. Acquisitions: Netscape made several acquisitions, including the purchase of Collabra Software in 1995. After its acquisition by AOL, Netscape was merged with the AOL Browser team to form the Mozilla Foundation in 2003. Awards and Achievements: Netscape Navigator was the dominant web browser in the mid-1990s, with market share reaching 90% by 1995. The company's successful IPO helped to kick-start the dot-com boom of the late 1990s.
