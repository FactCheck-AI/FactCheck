Actor Stephen Dunham has died of a heart attack, aged 48.

Dunham had suffered the cardiac arrest in Burbank, California, a few days before passing away on September 14.

The Boston-born actor had starred in films including Steven Spielberg's 'Catch Me If You Can', 'Monster-in-Law', 'The Mummy' and 'Traffic'.

He had also recently appeared in Oliver Stone's drug cartel thriller 'Savages', alongside Benicio Del Toro and Blake Lively.

Dunham was also a prolific TV actor, playing Edward Pillows in all 17 episodes of US show 'DAG', and as Dr Charlie Thorpe in 'Hot Properties'.

He was married to actress Alexondra Lee.
