“We wanted to avoid doing our dissertations” — Jerry Yang

When the founders created Yahoo it was a side project and a diversion from writing their dissertations at Stanford University. It was made to collect technical papers and areas they were interested in.

But soon their directory took on a life of it’s own and within a few short years became a massive corporation used by millions of people across the world.

Here is the story of how Yahoo became a dotcom giant.

Yahoo started as a diversion

The founders of Yahoo, Jerry Yang and David Filo were PhD students at Stanford University.
