Historical Background Of Beyoglu

Beyoğlu is one of Istanbul’s most vibrant and cosmopolitan districts, situated on the European side of the city. It is home to a diverse range of people, from artists and musicians to students and professionals. The area has a rich history, dating back to the Ottoman Empire.

During the Ottoman era, Beyoglu was known as Pera, which derives from the Greek word for “across” or “beyond”. It was here that European traders would come to do business with the Ottomans, and it quickly became a melting pot of cultures. The first foreign consulate in Istanbul was established in Pera in 1580, and over time more followed suit. This made Beyoğlu an important hub for diplomacy and trade.

In 1843, Pera was connected to Galata by a bridge, making it even more accessible to foreigners. This led to an influx of tourists, many of whom were drawn to its cafes and nightclubs. Beyoğlu developed a reputation for being a lively and exotic place, which it still retains today.

The 20th century saw some turbulent times for Beyoğlu. During World War I, many of its residents were forced to flee as the city came under Allied bombardment. In 1955, there were widespread riots following the murder of a Turkish diplomat in Greece. However, these events did not dampen the district’s spirit; it remains one of the most popular Istanbul areas, both with locals and visitors alike.

Galata Mevlevihanesi

Galata Mevlevihanesi is a beautiful Turkish Sufi house of worship located in Beyoglu, Istanbul. The building was constructed in 1491 and is one of the city’s most important historical landmarks. The Mevlevi Order, also known as the whirling dervishes, is a mystical Islamic sect that was founded in the 13th century by the poet and scholar Rumi.

The order believes in the spiritual unity of all things, and practitioners perform a ceremonial dance known as the Sema, which symbolizes the cycle of life, death, and rebirth. Visitors to the Galata Mevlevihanesi can watch the Sema ceremony or take part in workshops where they can learn about the history and philosophy of the Mevlevi Order.
