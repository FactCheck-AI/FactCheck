Women’s Basketball Adds Three to Class of 2025

Two Guards and One Forward Set to Join Coach Adeduntan’s Squad

HAMILTON – Head Women's Basketball Coach Ganiyat Adeduntan has announced the Class of 2025, featuring three incoming first-years who are set to join the program this season.

The Class of 2025 includes two guards and one forward hailing from two different states who look to make an immediate impact on the program in the 2021-22 season.

"I am extremely excited about our incoming class," Adeduntan said. "We feel that they all value hard work and integrity and have a commitment to excellence both on and off the court."

The three first-years are listed below in alphabetical order.

Sophia Diehl

6-1 | Forward

Westminster, Md. | Roland Park County School

Before Colgate

Westminster High School in Westminster, Md., as freshman and sophomore … Sophomore-year captain; Roland Park County School in Baltimore as junior and senior … Senior-year captain.

Stats: Scored 930 points for career … During five-game senior season, averaged 18.8 points, 9.0 rebounds, 2.2 steals and 1.6 blocks while shooting 44.8 percent from field, 45.2 percent from 3-point and 80 percent at line … As junior, averaged 12.2 points and 6.8 rebounds … As sophomore, averaged 11.0 points, 8.6 rebounds and 1.4 steals … As freshman, averaged 10.6 points, 5.1 rebounds and 1.4 steals…Also played volleyball freshman season … Played for Maryland Belles in AAU basketball ... Team captain … National Honor Society member ... FCA, Key Club, Yearbook and Zen Club.

Honors: Two-time All-County First Team … All-Metro Second Team … Helped Westminster to Carroll County League championship in 2017-18 and regional final in 2018-19.

Personal

Born: Sophia Corinne Diehl in Westminster, Maryland.

Family: Parents are Ben and Sherri Diehl … Has two brothers.

Taylor Golembiewski

5-8 | Guard

Columbia, Conn. | Edwin O. Smith

Before Colgate

Edwin O. Smith High School in Storrs, Conn. … Senior-year captain and junior-year tri-captain.

Stats: As senior, averaged 12.0 points, 4.6 rebounds, 2.6 assists, 2.9 steals and 0.5 blocks … For career, averaged 17.1 points, 5.1 rebounds, 2.8 assists, 4.3 steals and 0.7 blocks…Played for Connecticut Attack in AAU basketball ... Ran multiple skill development clinics and sessions for that club and served as assistant coach for sixth-grade Attack team in 2020-21.

Honors: Nominated as 2020-21 Connecticut Girls Basketball Gatorade Player of the Year … Two-time CHSCA Class LL All-State … Three-time All-Central Connecticut Conference … Helped Panthers to 2020 and 2021 Central Connecticut Conference titles.

Personal

Born: Taylor Anne Golembiewski in Hartford, Connecticut.

Family: Parents are Brian and Leigh Golembiewski … Has one sister.

Morgan McMagon

5-10 | Guard

Hagerstown, Md. | Saint James School

Before Colgate

Saint James School in Hagerstown, Md.

Stats: Averaged 12.8 points and 6.0 assists and 2.8 steals for career ... As senior, averaged 16.3 points, 6.0 assists and 2.5 steals…Also lettered in lacrosse and cross country ... Played for the Germantown Lady Panthers club basketball team ... Member of Prefect Council.

Honors: Four-time Herald Mail All-County, All-WCAA Private School and IPSL-All-Conference ... McDonald's All-American nominee ... Earned team Coaches' Award ... Helped Saints to IPSL championship in 2018, 2019 and 2020, and to Title IX Tournament championship in 2019 and 2020.

Personal

Born: Morgan Francis McMahon in Hagerstown, Maryland.

Family: Parents are Todd and Carrie McMahon … Has one sister.

"Each of these young women have traits we value," Adeduntan said. "They are competitive, coachable, and selfless players who have a high level of energy and enthusiasm. They all bring versatility in their positions and understand the importance of competing on both ends of the floor. We can't wait to get to work with them!"
