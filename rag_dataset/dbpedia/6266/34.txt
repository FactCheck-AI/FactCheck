Biography

Monroe Trotter is Notable.

William Monroe Trotter was a newspaper editor, civil rights activist, and real estate businessman. He was an early opponent of the accommodationist race policies of Booker T. Washington. He was one of the founders of the NAACP. In 1905, he was a part of starting and leading the Niagara Movement, a civil rights organization.[1]

William Monroe Trotter was born in 1872 in Chillicothe, Ohio to Virginia Isaacs and Civil War veteran James Monroe Trotter of the 55th Massachusetts Volunteer Infantry. His father's mother, Letitia, was an enslaved Black woman in Claiborne County, Mississippi and his father's father, Richard Monroe, was the white man who enslaved his grandmother. He was also a descendent of the Hemings family of Monticello on his mother's side.

In 1889, he married Geraldine L. Pindell.[2] They had no children.

Attended Harvard University

1st man of color to earn a Phi Beta Kappa key at Harvard

He passed away in 1934.
