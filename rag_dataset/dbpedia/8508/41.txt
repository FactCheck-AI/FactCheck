Kevin Michael Allin, professionally known as GG Allin and born as Jesus Christ Allin, (August 29, 1956 â€“ June 28, 1993) was an American punk rock musician and songwriter who performed and recorded with many groups during his career. Allin was best known for his controversial live performances, which often featured transgressive acts, including setting himself on fire and assaulting audience members, for which he was arrested and imprisoned on multiple occasions.

Known for his controversial stage antics, Allin mostly recorded punk rock albums, although he did release spoken word and country music albums as well.

Allin released many albums throughout his career starting with his debut album Always Was, Is And Always Shall Be. Allin began included shock value in his lyrics with his second album Eat My Fuc. Allin then formed many solo bands starting with GG Allin And The Scumfucs, with whom he released his third album with, You'll Never Tame Me. Completion album Hated In The Nation was released to help give Allin more notoriety. Allin formed another solo band called GG Allin and the Holy Men, with whom he released his fourth album with, You Give Love A Bad Name. Allin's fifth album, Freaks, F****ts, Drunks And Junkies, continued his shock rock themes, most prominently in the album title. Allin's sixth album The Suicide Sessions was released before Allin was arrested on assault charges. Allin's seventh album, Murder Junkies was released in collaboration with punk rock band Antiseen. Allin would release his final album, Brutality And Bloodshed For All shortly before his death in 1993.

Allin promised for several years that he would commit suicide on stage during one of his concerts, but instead died from an accidental drug overdose on June 28, 1993, at the age of 36.
