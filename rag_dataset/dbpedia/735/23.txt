(born: 1912 - died: 2002)

Born in Washington, D.C., Benjamin O. Davis, Jr. was the first African American to command an airbase, Godman Field, KY, in 1945-46. He was also the first African American Brigadier General in the Air Force, obtaining the rank of a three-star general before retiring in 1970. His father was the first African American General in the Army.

For more see Benjamin O. Davis, Jr., American: an Autobiography, by B. O. Davis.
