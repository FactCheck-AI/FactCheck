Anne Mazer is the author of The Amazing Days of Abby Hayes "The Salamander Room," and The No-Nothings and Their Baby. She was born in 1953 in Schenectady, New York. As the daughter of novelists Harry Mazer and Norma Fox Mazer, Mazer loved books and said that she would often sneak out of high school to go to the public library. After high school, she went on to study art, at Syracuse University's School of Visual and Performing Arts. She also studied French language and literature at the Sorbonne in Paris, where she lived for three years.

Mazer began writing in Paris and her first published work was Watch Me.
