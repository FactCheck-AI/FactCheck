Biography

Melvin Edward Webb is the son of Roy Webb and Mildred Bottom.[1][2]

Born April 11, 1935 in Burney, he was the son of Roy and Mildred (Bottom) Webb, both deceased. He married Barbara Belin Aug. 9, 1975; she survives.

Mr. Webb lived his early life in Rush County. He was employed in construction most of his life. He served on the executive board of the IUOE International Union of Electrical Engineers, was a veteran of the Korean War (1952-1955), was a Master Mason and belonged to the Ancient Arabic Order of the Noble and Mystic Shrine.

Survivors in addition to his wife include son, Michael (Sheila) Webb of Huntington Beach, Calif.; step daughter, Debbie (Todd) Amundson of Elm Grove, Wis..; brothers, Charles Webb and Jack Webb, both of Rushville, and Robert Webb of Bloomington; sisters, Imogene Morris of Connersville and Barbara Carol Hamilton of Rushville; and grandsons, Logan and Zachary.

Besides his parents he was preceded in death by a brother, Keith Webb.

Services will be at 1 p.m. Friday at Moster Mortuary in Rushville with the Rev. Charles Justice officiating. Burial will follow in Rushville East Hill Cemetery and will include a graveside military service. Visitation will be after 11:30 a.m. Friday at the mortuary.[3]

Melvin E. Webb, 73, of Huntington Beach, Calif., died Jan. 15, 2009.[4]
