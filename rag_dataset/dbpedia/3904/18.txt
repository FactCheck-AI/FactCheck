I’m still down the National Film Board rabbit hole and came across this documentary on their YouTube page.

Death of a Legend explores the myths around wolves in Canada. This film asks if wolves are pesky predators, killing indiscriminately or misunderstood animals that help bring balance to ecosystems.

Some of this footage is pretty spectacular and some of it will be disturbing for some people (shooting wolves, etc) but it feels so distinctly Canadian.

Watching this film made me feel like there are probably SO MANY fantastic Canadian filmmakers who never got the recognition and support they deserved. What could the state of Canadian films be today if the industry had been supported and invested in decades ago?
