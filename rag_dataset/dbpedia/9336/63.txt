Paul G. Randolph Edit Profile

Paul G. Randolph, American history educator, minister. Faculty sponsor Pepperdine University Young Democrats, since 1980; member Woodland Hills Church of Christ, since 1984. With United States Army, 1946-1947. Member Phi Beta Kappa, Phi Alpha Theta, Pi Gamma Museum.

Background

Randolph, Paul G. was born on October 10, 1927 in Henderson, Tennessee, United States. Son of Paul Dorris and Clara Cathleen (Titsworth) Randolph.

Education

Bachelor with highest honors, University of Illinois, 1950; Master of Arts, University of Illinois, 1951; Doctor of Philosophy, University of Michigan, 1972.

Career

Instructor, Freed-Hardeman U., Henderson, 1951-1953; minister, Redford Church of Christ, Detroit, 1953-1955; minister, Hayes Avenue Church of Christ, Detroit, 1956-1958; assistant professor, Pepperdine U., Los Angeles, 1958-1970; professor, Pepperdine U., Malibu, since 1978; from associate professor to professor, Morehead (Kentucky) State University, 1970-1978.

Achievements

Membership

Faculty sponsor Pepperdine University Young Democrats, since 1980. Member Woodland Hills Church of Christ, since 1984. With United States Army, 1946-1947.
