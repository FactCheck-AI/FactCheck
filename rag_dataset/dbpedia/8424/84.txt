Nicosia, Cyprus

Latitude and longitude coordinates are: 35.185566, 33.382275.

Nicosia or Lefkosia (Λευκωσία) is the largest and the most populous city in Cyprus, it is the capital and divided area of a territorial dispute between Turkey and Greece. The city is truly beautiful architecturally, with plenty of old buildings preserved since the times of Byzantine and Medieval times, with a number of beautiful mosques, churches, parks, and entertainment facilities. Since the area was under the rule of the British, there are a number of UK-related traditions and small lifestyle tricks, like left-side traffic, etc. Archbishop's Palace, Girne Kapısı, Lapidary Museum, Cyprus Folk Art Museum, Cyprus Classic Motorcycle Museum, The Cyprus Museum, Leventis Municipal Museum of Nicosia, Cypriot Archeological Museum, NiMac, SPEL - The State Gallery of Modern and Contemporary Cypriot Art, The Hadjigeorgakis Kornesios Mansion, Lusignan House, Venedik Sütunu, Saint Sophia Cathedral, St. John's Cathedral, Famagusta Gate, and Omeriye Mosque are among the historical landmarks and attractions.
