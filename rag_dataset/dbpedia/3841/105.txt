ISLE ROYALE, Mich. (Northern News Now) - According to the Wall Street Journal, the best uncrowded national park in the country is right here in our neck of the woods.

They recently named Isle Royale, located on Lake Superior, as their top pick for national parks that aren’t too busy.

Tourism bureau leaders from Keweenaw County said this recognition highlights guests ability to really connect with nature while visiting.

Isle Royale National Park is unique in that it’s only accessible by seaplane or ferry.

About 20,000 people visit the park each summer.

Isle Royale beat out other parks like Big Bend, Denali, and Yellowstone.
