The Mission is a 1986 British period drama film about the experiences of a Jesuit missionary in 18th-century South America. Directed by Roland Joffé and written by Robert Bolt, the film stars Robert De Niro, Jeremy Irons, Ray McAnally, Aidan Quinn, Cherie Lunghi, and Liam Neeson.

Samuel Fritz SJ (9 April 1654 – 20 March 1725, 1728 or 1730) was a Czech Jesuit missionary, noted for his exploration of the Amazon River and its basin. He spent most of his life preaching to Indigenous communities in the western Amazon region, including the Omaguas, the Yurimaguas, the Aisuare, the Ibanomas, and the Ticunas. In 1707 he produced the first accurate map of the Amazon River, establishing as its source the Marañón.

James Fanstone (8 August 1890 to 1985) was a British Christian medical missionary. In the early 1900s he traveled into the interior of Brazil to live and work as a surgeon and doctor in the remote town of Anapolis in the Brazilian state of Goias, a remote region which had a population of over 500,000, but had no doctors and no schools. He built the first hospital in the region.
