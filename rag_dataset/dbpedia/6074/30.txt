Ed Llewellyn, peerage

David Cameron’s loyal chief of staff in opposition and in No 10, the 51-year-old was two years above the former prime minister at Eton. Contemporaries at Oxford University. He was one of the party’s four coalition negotiators after the 2010 election. Mr Cameron reportedly dubbed him “the pocket Talleyrand”.

Camilla Cavendish, peerage

Another Oxford contemporary, the former Times and Sunday Times journalist read PPE at Brasenose College with Mr Cameron. After the 2015 general election, she became the head of the Downing Street policy unit, quitting last month when Theresa May became prime minister.
