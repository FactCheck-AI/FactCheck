Maria Vittoria dal Pozzo (Maria Vittoria Carlotta Enrichetta; 9 August 1847 – 8 November 1876) was born an Italian noblewoman who later became queen consort of Spain after her husband, previously the Duke of Aosta was created King Amedeo I of Spain in 1870 until his husband's abdication in 1873. On July 7, 1871 the Civil Order of Maria Victoria was created in her honour.

Marriage[edit]

On 30 May 1867 she married Prince Amedeo of Savoy, Duke of Aosta.

Children[edit]

Prince Emanuele Filiberto of Savoy, Duke of Aosta (13 January 1869 – 4 July 1931) married Princess Hélène of Orléans and had children.

Prince Vittorio Emanuele of Savoy, Count of Turin (24 November 1870 – 10 October 1946), never married.

Prince Luigi Amedeo of Savoy, Duke of the Abruzzi (29 January 1873 – 18 March 1933) never married.
