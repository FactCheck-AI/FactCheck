U.S. Military Bases by State: Overview

Our military bases located within the borders of the United States serve a number of purposes, the most obvious of which is to defend our sovereignty. Each service maintains numerous installations across the country, and each installation has a separate mission. Military members stationed at a military base in the United States is said to be serving a Contiguous United States (CONUS) assignment. This includes only those military bases located within the lower 48 states. Therefore, Hawaii and Alaska are not considered to be CONUS assignments.
