Dick Van Dyke biography

Richard Wayne Van Dyke was born on 13th December 1925 in West Plains, Missouri, USA. He is the son of Hazel Victoria (mother) and Loren Wayne Van Dyke (father). His father was a salesman, while his mother was a stenographer.

He was raised in Danville, Illinois, alongside his elder brother Jerry Van Dyke. Jerry was an American actor, musician and comedian. He was born on 27th July 1931 and passed away on 5th January 2018 at his Hot Spring County, Arkansas ranch from heart failure.

The actor is Dutch and has English, Irish and Scottish ancestry. His family lineage traces back to the Mayflower passenger, John Alden. He attended the Danville High School, but there are no details about his higher education.

Career

In 1944, he left high school during his senior year and joined the United States Army Air Forces for pilot training. He was denied enlistment on several occasions because he was underweight. Eventually, he was accepted for service as a radio announcer.

During the late 1940s, after his military service, Dick worked as a radio DJ in Danville, Illinois. In 1947 he teamed up with Phil Erickson to form a comedy duo titled Eric and Van-the Merry Mutes. The duo toured West Coast Nightclub circuit. They also performed at several TV shows.

In 1959, he made his Broadway debut in The Girls Against the Boys. He was the lead actor in Broadway, Bye Bye Birdie. It started on 14th April 1960 and came to an end on 7th October 1961. Later, he appeared in another Broadway, Revival the Music Man.

Van Dyke began his TV career with WDSU-TV New Orleans Channel 6, with his first performance happening in 1964 with Dennis James. He worked with The New Dick Van Dyke Show from 1971 to 1974. Later, he worked as the voice actor for the New Scooby-Doo Movies, and Scooby-Doo Meets Dick Van Dyke. Other Dick Van Dyke's movies and TV shows include:

Wife and children

Who is Dick Van Dyke's wife and children? Initially, the actor was married to Margie Willet. The duo tied the knot on 12th February 1948 at the Chapman Park Hotel on Wilshire Boulevard in Los Angeles, California.

The couple was blessed with four lovely children, Barry Van Dyke, Stacy Van Dyke, Christian Van Dyke, and Carrie Beth van Dyke. They separated for a long time before divorcing in 1984. In 1976, the actor started dating his longtime companion, Michele Triola Marvin. The two stayed together for over 30 years until she passed away in 2009.

On 29th February 2012, at the age of 86, the actor exchanged his wedding vows with Arlene Silver. The duo had met six years earlier during the Screen Actors Guild (SAG) Awards.

Dick Van Dyke's health condition and alcoholism

Van Dyke struggled with alcoholism in the past. In 1972, he checked into a hospital for three weeks for addiction treatment. He was a heavy smoker for the bigger part of his adult life. In April 2013, he revealed that for seven years, he was experiencing symptoms of neurological disorder.

He felt a pounding in his head every time he lied down. Despite taking numerous tests, no diagnosis was made. In May 2013, he tweeted that his titanium dental implants were the cause of his health condition.
