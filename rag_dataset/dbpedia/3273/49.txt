The Brazil national football team, nicknamed The Seleção, represents Brazil in men's international football. They are the most successful national team in World Cup history (5 titles) and they have a massive fan following all over the world. They have been a member of CONMEBOL (South America Football Confederation) since 1916 and of FIFA since 1923.

Brazil have been crowned winners of Copa America 9 times and the Confederations Cup 4 times. The Brazil national football team is the only national team to have played in all World Cup editions without any absence. The Seleção also has a record 74 victories in 110 matches played at the World Cup over the years.

Brazil is currently ranked No. 1 in the latest FIFA rankings. Brazil's football team has notable rivalries with its South American neighbors Argentina and Uruguay, and the European nation of Italy. Adenor Leonardo Bacchi, commonly known as Tite, has been the head coach of the Brazil national football team since 2016.

The 26-player Brazil squad contains 3 goalkeepers, 8 defenders, 6 midfielders, and 9 forwards.

The Brazil football team has been one of the most successful and consistent teams in the history of the Football World Cup. Since participating in the very first edition of the World Cup in 1930, Brazil have been part of every tournament played.

In the 22 appearances in the Football World Cup so far, the Brazil football team has 5 title wins, and finished runners-up, third place and fourth place twice each.

Winners: 1958, 1962, 1970, 1994, 2002

Runners-up: 1950, 1998

Third place: 1938, 1978

Fourth place: 1974, 2014

Brazil Team in the Copa America

In the 37 appearances in the Copa America so far, Brazil football team has 9 title wins, and finished runners-up 12 times, third place 7 times, and fourth place 3 times.

Winners: 1919, 1922, 1949, 1989, 1997, 1999, 2004, 2007, 2019

Runners-up: 1921, 1925, 1937, 1945, 1946, 1953, 1957, 1959, 1983, 1991, 1995, 2021

Third place: 1916, 1917, 1920, 1942, 1959, 1975, 1979

Fourth place: 1923, 1956, 1963

Brazil Team in the Confederations Cup

In the seven appearances in the now discontinued Confederations Cup, the Brazil football team has 4 title wins, and finished runners-up and fourth place once each.

Winners: 1997, 2005, 2009, 2013

Runners-up: 1999

Fourth place: 2001
