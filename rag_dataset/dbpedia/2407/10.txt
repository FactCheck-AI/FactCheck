This guide lists Attorneys-General for each jurisdiction in Australia. The first Attorney-General was Saxe Bannister who was appointed in the colony of New South Wales in 1823. Links to authoritative biographical information are given for federal Attorneys-General. For an overview of the role of the Attorney-General see the 1997 Report on the Review of the Attorney-General's Legal Practice. Chapter 3. Role of the Attorney-General.

Attorneys-General are also listed from 1890 in A Handbook of Australian Government and Politics, by Colin Hughes, (Canberra: Australian National University Press) covering the periods 1890-1964, 1965-1974, 1975-1984 and 1985-1999.
