Ella Wishes You a Swinging Christmas (1960)

"Ella Wishes You a Swinging Christmas" is a holiday album by the legendary jazz vocalist Ella Fitzgerald, released in 1960. Known for her pure tone, impeccable diction, phrasing, timing, and intonation, Ella brings her distinctive style to this joyful collection of classic Christmas tunes. The album stands out as one of the finest holiday jazz albums ever recorded.

A Jazz-Infused Christmas

This festive album features a variety of well-known holiday songs, each infused with a unique jazz flavor that only Ella could provide. Tracks like “Jingle Bells,” “Santa Claus Is Coming to Town,” and “Winter Wonderland” are delivered with Ella’s swinging, upbeat style, making them feel fresh and vivacious.

The Standout Tracks

Ella's rendition of “Have Yourself a Merry Little Christmas” is a highlight of the album, showcasing her ability to convey deep emotion in her performance. Another notable track, “What Are You Doing New Year’s Eve?”, is a masterclass in vocal jazz, with Ella’s voice effortlessly conveying the song’s romantic longing.

Collaboration with Renowned Arrangers

The album features arrangements by Frank DeVol, a respected arranger and composer, who provides the perfect backdrop to Ella's vocals. His work ensures that the album swings in a way that is both sophisticated and accessible, making it appealing to both jazz aficionados and casual listeners.

Legacy and Influence

"Ella Wishes You a Swinging Christmas" has remained a beloved holiday album over the decades, cherished for its ability to capture the joy and warmth of the Christmas season. It showcases Ella Fitzgerald's versatility as a singer and her ability to imbue traditional Christmas songs with her unique jazz sensibility.

A Must-Have for the Holiday Season

For fans of jazz and holiday music, “Ella Wishes You a Swinging Christmas” is a must-have in any music collection. The album is not only a festive treat but also a testament to Ella Fitzgerald's enduring charm and unmatched talent as one of the greatest jazz vocalists of all time.
