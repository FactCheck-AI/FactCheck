History

ViacomCBS was formed in 2019 through the merger of media conglomerates Viacom and CBS Corporation.

Prior to the merger, Viacom was founded in 1952 as a subsidiary of National Amusements, Inc., which is controlled by Sumner Redstone. The company’s initial focus was on producing and distributing films, but it later expanded into television production and broadcasting. In 1971, Viacom became an independent company after being spun off from National Amusements.

CBS Corporation, meanwhile, was established in 1986 when Paramount Pictures and CBS Television Studios were merged together under the name Columbia Pictures Entertainment Group. Over time, the company acquired other assets such as the CBS Radio network and the rights to the NFL’s broadcasting contracts. It also owned several TV stations and cable networks before rebranding itself as CBS Corporation in 2003.

In 2019, Viacom and CBS Corporation announced that they would be merging back together to form ViacomCBS. The new company combined Viacom’s film and TV studio operations with CBS’s broadcasting properties, creating one of the largest media companies in the world.

Current state and ownership structure

ViacomCBS is currently one of the largest media companies in the world, with operations spanning across various industries such as television networks, film production, publishing, and more.

As for its ownership structure, ViacomCBS is majority-owned by National Amusements Inc., which is controlled by the Redstone family through a trust. The remaining shares are publicly traded on the NASDAQ stock exchange under the ticker symbol VIAC.

Business segments and subsidiaries

ViacomCBS has several business segments and subsidiaries.

The company operates in three main segments: Media Networks, Paramount Pictures, and Publishing.

Media Networks includes cable networks such as MTV, VH1, Nickelodeon, and BET. It also includes digital media properties like Comedy Central, Spike TV, and TV Land.

Paramount Pictures is one of the largest film studios in Hollywood, producing blockbuster movies like Transformers, Mission: Impossible, and Star Trek.

Publishing includes Simon & Schuster, which publishes books under various imprints including Penguin Random House.

In addition to these segments, ViacomCBS also owns numerous subsidiaries, including CBS Television Studios, CBS Interactive, and Showtime Networks.
