Warner Music Group (WMG) is a global music company that operates in over 70 countries, and is headquartered in New York City. It was founded in 1958 by Warner Bros. Records and has since grown into one of the largest music conglomerates in the world. WMG’s business includes recorded music, music publishing, and artist services. The company’s recorded music division includes a roster of well-known artists such as Ed Sheeran, Bruno Mars, Cardi B, Coldplay, and Dua Lipa, as well as legendary acts like Led Zeppelin and Fleetwood Mac. The music publishing division includes a large catalog of songs, representing numerous genres including pop, rock, hip-hop, and country. WMG has a strong focus on innovation, investing heavily in technology and data analytics to help artists reach wider audiences and create new revenue streams. The company has partnerships with major digital platforms such as YouTube, Spotify, and Apple Music, and has pioneered new formats such as VR concerts and livestream events. Today, WMG is one of the “big three” music companies alongside Universal Music Group and Sony Music Entertainment. It is owned by Access Industries, a privately held multinational industrial group led by American billionaire Len Blavatnik.
