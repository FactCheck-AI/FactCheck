HARRISBURG, Pa. — Carlton Sherwood, a Vietnam War veteran and former journalist who produced a film attacking Democrat John F. Kerry during his 2004 presidential campaign, died of heart failure June 11 at the University of Pennsylvania Hospital. He was 67.

Mr. Sherwood was part of a Gannett News Service reporting team that won a Pulitzer Prize for public service in 1980.

His 2004 film ‘‘Stolen Honor: Wounds That Never Heal’’ alleged that Kerry’s actions as an antiwar activist after he returned home from Vietnam harmed American prisoners of war.

Attacks on Kerry’s own military record were later viewed as a factor in his election loss

Mr. Sherwood’s family said that he would receive a burial in Arlington National Cemetery.
