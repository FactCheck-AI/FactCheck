Kempe Gowda - Bangalore Founder

Bangalore started way back in the year 1537. An architect named Kempe Gowda built a fort using mud and named that mud the Vijayanagara Empire. With this, the city of Bangalore was born. A huge part of land was gifted to Kempe Gowda by the king. The land is today known as Bangalore. The name found its existence from the description in Ganga in the 9th century called Bengaluru.
