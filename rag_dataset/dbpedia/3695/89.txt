October 3

Women's Golf

at Butler Fall Invitational, All Day, Indianapolis, Indiana, Highland Country Club

The CSU women's golf team is scheduled to compete in the Butler Fall Invitational at the Highland Country Club (Par 70, 6,040 yards) in Indianapolis, Indiana. Teams will play 54 holes, 36 Monday and a final 18 on Tuesday. Participating teams include Butler, Austin Peay, Ball State, Bellarmine, Chicago State, Dayton, Evansville, UIC, Indiana State, IUPUI, Marshall, Northern Kentucky, Tennessee State, Valparaiso and Youngstown State.

Men's Golf

at Flyer Invitational hosted by Lewis University, All Day, Lemont, IL, Cog Hill #4 - Dubsdread

The Chicago State men's golf team will compete at the Flyer Invitational hosted by Lewis University at Cog Hill #4 – Dubsdread (Par 71, 7,554 yards) on October 3-4. The course hosted the PGA's Western Open and BMW Championship from 1991-2011 and Tiger Woods holds the course record with 9-under 62.

Volleyball

at Gonzaga, 8 p.m., Spokane, Washington, Charlotte Y. Martin Centre

The Chicago State volleyball team will play at Gonzaga on Tuesday, October 4 at 8 p.m. at the Charlotte Y. Martin Centre in Spokane, Washington. The Cougars are 5-8 and enter the contest with a two-match win streak following wins over Oakland and Albany. Gonzaga enters the match with a 3-12 record, have lost five consecutive matches and are 2-6 at home this season, with victories over Eastern Washington and Cal State-Northridge.

October 6

Men's Soccer

at Northern Illinois University, 5 p.m., Chicago, Illinois, Kroc Stadium

The Chicago State's men's soccer team will make their home Mid-American Conference (MAC) debut on Thursday, October 6 at 5 p.m. against NIU at Kroc Stadium in Chicago. The Cougars made their MAC debut Sunday at Western Michigan and enter the match with the Huskies with a 1-6-3, 0-1-0 MAC record following a 7-1 loss to the Broncos. The Huskies are 0-2 in MAC play and are 3-4-3 overall following a 4-1 loss to Akron Sunday.

Cougars fans can watch a live stream of the match HERE or follow along on LiveStats HERE.

Women's Soccer

at Southern Indiana, 7 p.m., Evansville, Indiana, Strassweg Field

The Chicago State women's soccer team will play Southern Indiana for the second time this season on Thursday, October 6 at 7 p.m. at Strassweg Field in Evansville, Indiana. The two teams played in Chicago September 9 with USI claiming a 2-0 victory. The Cougars are 3-10-1 following back-to-back games at Hartford where CSU lost a pair of games, losing 3-1 Sunday following a 4-0 setback last Friday. Southern Indiana enters the match with a 3-5-3 record following a 0-0 tie Sunday against SIUE. The Screaming Eagles are 1-2-0 at home this season and enter Thursday's matchup with a five-game winless streak, with four losses and a tie, including losing their last two at home.

Volleyball

October 9

Men's Soccer

vs. Akron, 5 p.m., Chicago, Illinois, Gwendolyn Brooks College Prep

The Chicago State's men's soccer team will play their third MAC contest of the week and second consecutive home match against Akron on Sunday, October 9 at Gwendolyn Brooks College Prep in Chicago. Akron enters the week with a 6-2-2 (1-0-0) record, playing their first MAC match at home against UNI Sunday where they beat the Huskies 4-1. The Zips will face Bowling Green Thursday on the road before traveling to Chicago for Sunday's match. Akron is ranked No. 25 in the United Soccer Coaches poll and are 2-2-2 against ranked teams this season, beating No. 18 Florida International (5-3) and No. 24 Duquesne (1-0), tying No. 22 Ohio State (3-3) and No. 3 Pittsburgh (3-3) while dropping matches to No. 11 Indiana (2-0) and No. 18 Penn State (1-0).

Cougar Pride Fund

The Cougar Pride Fund, an annual giving component benefiting student-athlete scholarships and sport program operations, is in its second year and offers a variety of membership levels. As a foundational-level member of the Cougar Pride Fund, members will be witness to the unfolding of an era of growth within Chicago State University's athletic programs. In order to take our next step in developing competitive programs, supporting our student-athletes through cost of attendance and sport program operations is crucial.
