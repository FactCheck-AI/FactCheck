Croatian state-owned oil pipeline operator Jadranski Naftovod said it has extended an oil storage deal with London-based BP Oil International Limited.

Janaf will store some 123,000 cubic metres of crude oil for BP Oil International in its terminal in Omisalj, on the island of Krk.

This will be effective from April 1, 2021 to March 31, 2022, it said in a filing to the Zagreb bourse. The value of the deal was not disclosed.

Janaf controls oil terminals on the island of Krk, as well as in Sisak, Virje, Slavonski Brod and Zitnjak, and operates 631 kilometres of pipelines. It has 1.94 million cubic metres of storage capacity for oil and 222,000 cubic metres for the storage of petroleum products.
