}} Prince Philippe of Belgium, Count of Flanders (Philippe Eugène Ferdinand Marie Clément Baudouin Léopold Georges; 24 March 1837 – 17 November 1905), was the third born and second surviving son of King Leopold I of Belgium and Louise of Orléans.

Marriage[edit]

On 25 April 1867, he married Princess Marie of Hohenzollern-Sigmaringen, daughter of Karl Anton von Hohenzollern (1811–1885) and his wife Josephine of Baden.

Issue[edit]

Prince Baudouin of Belgium (3 June 1869 – 23 January 1891) he died unmarried.

Princess Henriette of Belgium (30 November 1870 – 28 March 1948) married Prince Emmanuel, Duke of Vendôme and had issue.

Princess Joséphine Marie (30 November 1870 – 18 January 1871) twin of the above.

Princess Joséphine Caroline of Belgium (18 October 1872 – 6 January 1958) married Prince Karl Anton of Hohenzollern and had issue.

Prince Albert of Belgium (later King Abert I) (8 April 1875 – 17 February 1934); he married Duchess Elisabeth in Bavaria and had issue.

Titles and styles[edit]

24 March 1837 – December 16, 1840 His Royal Highness Prince Philippe of Belgium, Prince of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha.

December 16, 1840 – 17 November 1905 His Royal Highness The Count of Flanders, Prince of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha.
