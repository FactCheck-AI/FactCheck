Rudyard Kipling produced some of the most exceptional children’s stories and adventure novels of all time. In 1907, he was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature. Over the decades, his stories have remained in print and shaped the minds of readers all over the world. This collection includes some of his finest stories, such as “The Man Who Would be King” and “They”. Alongside the text are Howard Rogers’ brilliant illustrations. For any collector of Kipling or classic literature, it is an appealing volume.

This illustrated edition is in very good condition. In the original half faux leather boards, elaborately gilt stamped, fine. Gilt spine label. Raised bands. Text block very good with only minor rubbing to gilt edges. Marble decorated endpapers and bookmark sewn in. Howard Rogers illustrations superb.

A very good collector’s copy of the Stories of Rudyard Kipling.
