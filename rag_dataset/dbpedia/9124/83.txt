Understanding the âMade in PRCâ Country of Origin Marking

Before we dive into the reasons behind the continued use of "Made in PRC" labels, it's important to understand what a Country of Origin Marking is:

What is a Country of Origin Marking?

Country of origin marking is a label affixed or marked on products to indicate where they were manufactured or produced. The primary purpose of this marking is to help consumers make informed decisions in relation to which country the imported article was made.

Why are country of origin markings important?

The country of origin marking serves several purposes:

Consumer Information: They help consumers make informed purchasing decisions by providing clear information about where a product was manufactured or produced.

Trade Regulations: They assist in enforcing trade regulations and ensure that products comply with import and export requirements specific to their origin.

Protecting Domestic Industries: Governments may use these markings to support domestic industries and encourage consumers to buy local products.

Product Safety and Quality: These markings can be indicative of the quality and safety standards associated with products from certain regions, allowing consumers to assess potential risks or benefits.

Transparency and Accountability: Country of origin markings promote transparency in the supply chain and hold manufacturers and importers accountable for providing accurate information about the origin of their products.

"Made in PRC" Decoding the Label

"Made in PRC": The Legal Aspect

Is Made In PRC Legal and Allowed?

Labeling individual items as "Made in PRC" is legal on the Chinese manufacturer's end and has served as a clever way to get around anti-China market bias. However, it's important to note that, especially in international shipping and for importers, this may not always be legal.Â

For example, when shipping and importing products from China to the United States, the country's name, "China," must be clearly stated in English on the product and packaging to be compliant with import rules and regulations.

Can I Put "Made in PRC" instead of "Made in China"?

In the quest to address concerns related to "Made in China," some may consider using the abbreviation "Made in PRC" as an alternative. However, when it comes to country of origin markings specific for the United States, the answer is "no."

Customs and Border Protection, responsible for enforcing such regulations, has made it clear that the abbreviation "PRC" is not acceptable for country of origin marking purposes. This stance has been reiterated in multiple instances, including references to HQ 560693 (March 6, 1998), HQ 562901 (November 6, 2003), and HQ 727372 (March 18, 1985).

Why Manufacturers Choose "Made in PRC"

You might wonder why "Made in PRC" labels continue to grace products despite legal restrictions on international shipments. The answer lies in the practical limitations of customs and inspection procedures. Customs agencies across the world handle a staggering volume of packages each day, making it virtually impossible to inspect every individual shipment thoroughly. This limitation is often exploited by manufacturers, importers, and sellers who choose to label their products as "Made in PRC."

Moreover, the situation is even more pronounced when products are individually shipped from China rather than in bulk. In these cases, items are often dispatched through various courier services, making it exceedingly challenging for customs to inspect each package. The "Made in PRC" label may slip through the cracks, resulting in products that, while not compliant with country of origin regulations, continue to reach consumers with these discreet labels intact.Â
