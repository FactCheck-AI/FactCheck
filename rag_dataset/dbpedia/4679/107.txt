Tamil actor Vijay’s political party, Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam (TVK), will not contest the Vikravandi bye-election on July 10 but will focus on serving the people till the Tamil Nadu Assembly polls that are scheduled in 2026.

In a statement, TVK said it would focus on strengthening its internal structural and public outreach across Tamil Nadu in preparation for the 2026 Assembly polls.

"Thalapathy Vijay, president of Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam, clearly outlined our party's political stance in his inaugural address on February 2. He has announced that the party's policies, principles, and programmes will be unveiled at the party’s first state conference," TVK's General Secretary N Anand said in a press statement.

"Therefore, we officially state that the Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam will not contest any elections, including local body elections, in the interim period. The party will not participate in the Vikravandi Assembly by-election and will not support any party," the statement added.

Earlier, Tamil Nadu's principal opposition party, AIADMK, also announced that it would be boycotting the July 10 polls. The BJP has decided that its ally Pattali Makkal Katchi (PMK) to contest from Vikravandi as the NDA candidate.

Vikravandi is part of the Villupuram Lok Sabha constituency and the by-poll was announced following the death of DMK MLA N Pugazhenthi in April this year.
