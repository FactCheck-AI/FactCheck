Geography

Croatia is a country at the eastern Adriatic coast. The land has a total area of 56,590 km² (21,850 mi²) and a total coastline of 5,835 km (3,625.7 mi). This land area is approximately 225% of the area of Maryland. Croatia is thus the 26th biggest country in Europe and in terms of area ranked 128th worldwide. More than half of all residents (59%) live within cities. Every fifth inhabitant lives in Zagreb.

Croatia is comparatively low at an average elevation of 331 meters above sea level. The highest mountain peak (Dinara) is at 1,831 meters. The country has 1,246 partially uninhabited islands in the open sea. There are direct national borders with the five neighboring countries Bosnia and Herzegovina, Hungary, Montenegro, Serbia and Slovenia. The distance between New York City and the Capital Zagreb is about 6,910 km (4,294 mi).
