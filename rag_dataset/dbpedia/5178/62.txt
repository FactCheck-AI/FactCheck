Biography

David, 7th Earl of Glasgow was born on 31 May 1833. He was the son of Patrick Boyle and Mary Frances Elphinstone-Dalrymple.

He gained the rank of Captain in the Royal Navy in which he fought in both the Crimean War and the Second Opium War

He held the office of Member of Parliament (M.P.) He held the office of Justice of the Peace (J.P.) for Ayrshire. He held the office of Deputy Lieutenant (D.L.) for Ayrshire. He held the office of Governor-General and Commander-in-Chief of New Zealand between 1892 and 1897.

Titles:

He succeeded as the 7th Earl of Glasgow [S., 1703] on 23 April 1890.

He succeeded as the 7th Lord Boyle, of Kelburn, Stewartoun, Finnick, Largs and Dalry [S., 1699] on 23 April 1890.

He succeeded as the 7th Lord Boyle, of Stewartoun, Cumbraes, Fenwick, Largs and Dalry [S., 1703] on 23 April 1890.

He succeeded as the 7th Viscount Kelburn [S., 1703] on 23 April 1890.

He was created 1st Baron Fairlie of Fairlie, Ayrshire [U.K.] on 23 July 1897.

Honours:

He was appointed Knight Grand Cross, Order of St. Michael and St. George (G.C.M.G.) in 1892.

He married Dorothea Elizabeth Thomasina Hunter Blair, daughter of Sir Edward Hunter Blair of Blairquhan and Dunskey, 4th Bt. and Elizabeth Wauchope, on 23 July 1873.

David died on 13 December 1915 at age 82.
