The Malaysian Front (Malay: Barisan Rakyat Malaysia; abbrev: BRM) is a political coalition consisting of the National Solidarity Front (Barisan Solidariti Nasional), Liberal Solidarity Movement (Gerakan Solidariti Liberal) and Malaysia National Organisation (Pertubuhan Nasional Malaysia). It was founded in 2021 as a coalition of right and left wing parties, with it being the only political coalition currently in Malaysia since the dissolution of Gagasan Perpaduan in January 2021. It has since faced Parti Demokratik Malaysia as its major challenge since its formation.

On the 16th of August 2022, Prime Minister BIuvye formed a government after the 2022 Malaysian Political Crisis, consisting of Cabinet Ministers from the Barisan Rakyat Malaysia, marking it as Barisan Rakyat Malaysia's first time in government.

History

Formation

Barisan Rakyat Malaysia formed before the 14th General Elections originally with 4 parties in the coalition: the National Solidarity Front (Barisan Solidariti Nasional), Liberal Solidarity Movement (Gerakan Solidariti Liberal), Malaysia National Organisation (Pertubuhan Nasional Malaysia) and People's Unity Party (Parti Kesatuan Rakyat). However, PKR was removed from the coalition following an emergency meeting held by the coalition's Supreme Council which regarded PKR as "incredibly immature, inexperienced and not exposed to Malaysian politics" in a press statement.[1]
