George Harrison (1943-2001) was a British singer-songwriter, guitarist, and producer. He was the lead guitarist of The Beatles and a key figure in the 1960s counterculture. He released his solo albums on the Beatles' Apple Records label and Dark Horse Records. Key albums include All Things Must Pass (1970), Living in the Material World (1973), and Cloud Nine (1987). His final studio album, Brainwashed, was released posthumously in 2002. He won several Grammy Awards, an Oscar, and was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2004.
