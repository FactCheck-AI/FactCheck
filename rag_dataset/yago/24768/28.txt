Biography

Rex Harrison is Notable.

Sir Rex Harrison (5 March 1908 – 2 June 1990) was an English actor of stage and screen.

He won Tony Awards for his performance as Henry VIII in the play Anne of the Thousand Days in 1949, and the role of Professor Henry Higgins in the stage production of My Fair Lady in 1957. He reprised the role for the 1964 film version, which earned him a Golden Globe Award and Best Actor Oscar. Rex Harrison has two stars on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.

On 25 July 1989, Harrison was knighted by Queen Elizabeth II.

Sir Reginald Carey 'Rex' Harrison was born on 5 March 1908 at Derry House in Huyton, Lancashire, England, United Kingdom, son of William Reginald Harrison (1872–1948), a cotton broker and his wife Edith Mary Carey (1868–1953).[1]

Rex had two older sisters, Sylvia Mary (Harrison) Sackville Countess De La Warr, DBE (16 July 1903–10 June 1992) and Edith Marjorie Harrison (1900-1976).

He was educated at Birkdale preparatory school and Liverpool College. After a bout of childhood measles, Rex lost most of the sight in his left eye.

In World War II in the Royal Air Force (1942–1944) he reached the rank of Flight Lieutenant.

After the end of the war, Rex moved to Hollywood with his second wife Lilli Palmer and signed a contract with 20th Century Studios.

He was married six times:

Rex (24) married Ethel Marjorie Noel Collette Thomas (22) (born on 25 December 1910 in Upton upon Severn, Worcestershire, England; daughter of Major John Cyril Collette Thomas HM Army and Jessie Maud (Scott-Brown) Thomas) in 1933 in London, England. Married from 1933–1942, divorced.

Their known son was the actor/singer Noel John Christopher Harrison (29 January 1934–19 October 2013).

Rex (34) married Lilli Marie Peiser known under her stage name Lilli Palmer (28) (born on 24 May 1914 in Posen, Preußen, Deutschland; daughter of Alfred Peiser and Rose (Lismann) Peiser) on 25 January 1943 in Hollywood, Los Angeles, California, United States. Married from 1943–1957, divorced 6 February 1957.

Their known son was the novelist/playwright Carey Harrison.

Rex (48) married Justine Kay Kendall McCarthy (29) (born on 21 May 1927 in Withernsea, Yorkshire, England, United Kingdom; daughter of Terence Justin Kendall McCarthy and Gladys (Drewery) McCarthy) in 1957. Married from 1957–1959, her death.

Rex (52) married Rachel Roberts (33) (born on 20 September 1927 in Llanelli, Carmarthenshire, Wales, United Kingdom; daughter of Richard Rhys Roberts and Rachel Ann (Jones) Roberts) in 1961 in Genoa, Genova, Liguria, Italy. Married from 1962–1971, divorced.

Rex (63) married Hon. Joan Elizabeth Rees-Williams (35) (born on 1 May 1936 in Cardiff, Glamorgan, Wales, United Kingdom; daughter of Lt.-Col. David Rees-Williams and Alice Alexandra Constance (Wills) Rees-Williams) on 26 August 1971 in Long Island, New York, United States. Married from 1971–1975, divorced.

Three stepsons, Damian Harris, Jared Harris, and Jamie Harris.

Rex married 1978 in Manhattan, New York City, New York, United States Mercia Tinker. Married from 1978–1990, his death.

Rex passed away on 2 June 1990 of pancreatic cancer at his home in Manhattan, New York City, New York, United States, aged 82.[2]

He was cremated and some of his ashes being subsequently scattered in Portofino, Italia and the rest being scattered at his second wife Lilli Palmer's grave at Forest Lawn Memorial Park in Glendale, California, in the Commemoration Section, Map 1, Lot 4066, Space 2.
