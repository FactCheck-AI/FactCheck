Leopold II of Belgium (French : Léopold Louis Philippe Marie Victor, Dutch : Leopold Lodewijk Filips Maria Victor; 9 April 1835 – 17 December 1909) was the second King of the Belgians from 1865 to 1909, and the founder and sole owner of the exploitative Congo Free State from 1885 to 1908.

Issue[edit]

Princess Louise of Belgium (18 February 1858 – 1 March 1924) married Prince Philipp of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha and had issue.

Prince Léopold, Duke of Brabant (12 June 1859 – 22 January 1869) died from pneumonia before succeeding his father.

Princess Stéphanie of Belgium (21 May 1864 – 23 August 1945) married Rudolf, Crown Prince of Austria and had issue.

Princess Clémentine of Belgium (30 July 1872 – 8 March 1955) married Victor, Prince Napoléon and had issue.
