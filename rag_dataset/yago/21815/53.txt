Reborn – a film by Norodom Sihanouk – 2006

Original Title: Reborn

Subject: In 1952, HRH Princess Norodom Kantha Bopha, nicknamed “A-Mech”, the preferred daughter of King Norodom Sihanouk, died victim of a brutal leukaemia, aged only 4 years old. Upon his return to Cambodia from exile in China in the years 1990s, a charming young lady of the Cambodian upper class, visited Norodom Sihanouk and claimed that she was the reborn Princess Kantha Bopha. Based on these real events, Norodom Sihanouk wrote the scenario of this film which was made in North Korea in March 2006.

Lon Nol, Lon Non, Lonnolians – a film by Norodom Sihanouk - 2005

Original Title: Lon Nol, Lon Non, Lonnoliens

Subject: A film inspired in real events which took place in Cambodia from 1970 to 1975

Date: April 1995

Director: Norodom Sihanouk

Language of film: Khmer – no subtitles

Copy or original: Copy (1 DVD)

Quality: Excellent

Editorial note: This film of Norodom Sihanouk was inspired by three events that took place during the “Khmer Republic” of Lon Nol (1970-1975). The first one concerned a conversation by Lon Nol with the former US Vice-President Spiro Agnew, during which Lon Nol told his visitor that there was a “dragon living under the basement of Watt Phnom (the famous sanctuary located in a hill in the centre of Phnom Penh). The second event was the so-called existence of a “sacred spade” that would prevent the Khmers Rouges from taking over the Cambodian capital and also the re-sale by Lon Nol’s army of medicines, arms and military equipment to the Khmers Rouges. The last event was the activities of Lon Nol’s brother, Colonel Lon Non, who managed to ruin a number of Sino-Khmer jewellers by making demands of precious jewels and money from them. Some were forced to migrate to Hong Kong.
