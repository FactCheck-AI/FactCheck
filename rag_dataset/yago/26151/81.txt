Princess Maria Carolina Augusta of Bourbon-Two Sicilies (born 26 April 1822 in Vienna, Austrian Empire; died 6 December 1869 in Twickenham, London, England, United Kingdom was a Princess of Bourbon-Two Sicilies by birth and a princess of the House of Orléans through her marriage to Prince Henry of Orléans, Duke of Aumale. Maria Carolina was the daughter of Prince Leopold of Bourbon-Two Sicilies, Prince of Salerno and his wife (and niece) Archduchess Maria Clementina of Austria, daughter of Francis II, Holy Roman Emperor.

Maria Carolina remained inconsolable over the death of her eldest son, Louis Philippe, and died in exile at Twickenham in 1869 from infectious tuberculosis. She and her husband had a loving marriage and after the death of his wife, Henry preferred to remain a widower until his death in 1897.
