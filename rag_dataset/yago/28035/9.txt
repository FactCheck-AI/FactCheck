Henry IV of France (13 December 1553 – 14 May 1610) known as "Good King Henry", was King of Navarre (as Henry III) from 1572 till 1610 and King of France from 1589 till 1610 uniting the larger Kingdom of France to the smaller Kingdom of Navarre together in a personal union.

First marriage[edit]

On 18 August 1572, Henry married his second cousin Margaret of Valois. They had no issue.

Second marriage[edit]

On 17 December 1600, Henry married Marie de' Medici, daughter of Francesco I de' Medici, Grand Duke of Tuscany and Archduchess Joanna of Austria.

Issue[edit]

Louis XIII of France 27 September 1601 – 14 May 1643) married Anne of Austria and had issue;

Elisabeth of France (22 November 1602 – 6 October 1644) married Philip IV of Spain and had issue.

Christine of France 10 February 1606 - 27 December 1663) married Victor Amadeus I of Savoy and had issue.

Nicolas Henri of France, Duke of Orléans (April 16, 1607 - November 17, 1611) died in infancy.

Gaston of France, Duke of Orléans (24 April 1608 – 2 February 1660) marred Marie de Bourbon, Duchess of Montpensier and had issue before her death in childbirth, after he married Princess Marguerite of Lorraine in 1632 but had no surviving issue.

Henrietta Maria of France 25 November 1609 – 10 September 1669) married King Charles I of England and had issue.

Issue with Gabrielle d'Estrées[edit]

César de Bourbon, Duke of Vendôme (3 June 1594 – 22 October 1665) married Princess Françoise of Lorraine, Mademoiselle de Mercœur and had issue.

Catherine Henriette de Bourbon Mademoiselle de Vendôme (11 November 1596 – 20 June 1663) married Charles of Lorraine, 2nd Duke of Elbeuf and had issue.

Alexandre de Bourbon, Chevalier de Vendôme (19 April 1598 – 8 February 1629) never married.

Stillborn son (1599)
