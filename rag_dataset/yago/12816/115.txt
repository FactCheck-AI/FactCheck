TARTARIN DE TARASCON (1934) black and white, 102m.

Directed by Raymond Bernard, based on a very popular picaresque novel by Alphonse Daudet. The dialogue is sharply written by Marcel Pagnol.

This was actually the next step by Raymond Bernard right after making his grandest film LES MISERABLES (1934) after the Victor Hugo novel.

Because of delusions of grandeur while picturing himself as a daring marksman and hunter, Tartarin (Raimu) faces the improbable reality of having to travel to Africa to shoot a lion, or preferably many, to avoid losing his face in his home village of Tarascon.
