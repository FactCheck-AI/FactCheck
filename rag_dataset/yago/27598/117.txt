Who is Salman Rushdie?

Salman Rushdie was born on June 19, 1947, in Mumbai, India.

Salman is a veteran novelist who is known for combining magical realism with historical fiction to describe connections, disruptions, and migrations between Western and Easter civilizations located on the Indian subcontinent.

As a result of his stellar novels, he has won several accolades such as the Booker Prize he won in 1981 for his second novel Midnight's Children.

Salman was also appointed a Commandeur de l'Ordre des Arts et des Lettres of France in 1999.

Subsequently, he was knighted for his services to literature in 2007.
