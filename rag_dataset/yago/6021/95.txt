Former EastEnders Actor Dies

EastEnders star Roberta Taylor has died at the age of 76.

The actor, best known for playing Irene Raymond in the BBC soap from 1997 to 2000, joined the cast of ITV’s The Bill as Inspector Gina Gold.

Taylor’s obituary in The Guardian said she died on 6 July from an infection caused by a fall two months prior, agitating her underlying health problems with pneumonia.

She leaves behind her actor husband Peter Guinness, her son Elliott, a granddaughter and two stepbrothers.

Her last major TV appearance came on the private investigators comedy Shakespeare & Hathaway.

Her stint in fictional began in 1997 when she arrived as the matriarch of the Hills family, visiting her children Sarah (Daniela Denby-Ashe) and Tony (Mark Homer).
