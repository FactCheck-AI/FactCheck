Biography

Ann (Lake) Sothern is Notable.

Ann Sothern was an American stage, radio, film, and television, actress whose career spanned nearly six decades. In the early 1950s,she starred in the television show, Private Secretary, followed by her own sitcom, The Ann Sothern Show. From 1965 to 1966, she provided the voice of Gladys Crabtree, the title character in the sitcom My Mother the Car. In 1987 she appeared in her final film, The Whales of August, starring Bette Davis and Lillian Gish. for which she received an Oscar nomination for Best Supporting Actress

Ann Sothern was born Harriette Arlene Lake 22 Jan 1909, in Valley City, Barnes, North Dakota. She was the eldest daughter of Walter J. Lake and Annette Yde, a concert singer who later worked as a vocal coach for Warner Bros. Studios. She had two younger sisters, Marion and Bonnie. Her maternal grandfather was Danish violinist Hans Nielsen.

Ann married actor and band leader Roger Pryor in September 1936. They separated in September 1941 and Sothern filed for divorce in April 1942, charging Pryor with mental cruelty.Their divorce became final in May 1943. Less than a week after her divorce from Pryor she married actor Robert Sterling. The couple had one daughter, Patricia Ann "Tisha" Sterling, before divorcing in March 1949.

Ann passed away 15 Mar 2001, in Ketchum, Blaine, Idaho.[1]
