Fernand Rivers (1879-1960) was a French actor, screenwriter, film producer, and director. In 1935, he produced and supervised the first two feature films directed by Sacha Guitry. Rivers pursued a career as a director until 1950.

Fernand Rivers was born François Large in 1879 in Saint-Lager, France. He was the older brother of the actor Rivers Cadet. Frenand started out as a theatre actor. Rivers was hired for the cinema in 1902 by Charles Pathé. At Pathé Frères, he starred in such silent shorts as Les Martyrs de l'Inquisition/Martyrs of the Inquisition (Lucien Nonguet, 1905) and the fairytale La peine du talion/Tit-for-Tat (Gaston Velle, 1906). He appeared with Charles Prince in several Rigadin comedies like Rigadin et la jolie manucure/Rigadin and the pretty manicure (Georges Mova, 1909). With Pathé, he created a comic character named 'Plouf', that became the hero of a series of films that he directed himself from 1915. An example is Plouf fait son voyage de noces à Deauville/Plouf made his honeymoon in Deauville (Fernand Rivers, 1917).

In 1920, Fernand Rivers stopped working as an actor, but he continued to direct and in 1933, he also became a producer. In 1933, he directed the film Le Maître de forges/The Ironmaster (Fernand Rivers, 1933) starring Gaby Morlay and Henry Rollan. The drama was scripted and supervised by Abel Gance. It was a remake of the silent film Le Maître de forges (1912). In 1948 Rivers himself remade the film, Le Maître de forges/The Ironmaster (Fernand Rivers, 1948), now starring Hélène Perdrière and Jean Chevrier In 1935 Rivers and Gance co-directed the biographical drama Pasteur (Sacha Guitry, Fernand Rivers, 1935) starring Guitry, Maurice Schutz, and Gaston Dubosc. It portrays the life of the French scientist Louis Pasteur. Rivers then directed his brother Rivers Cadet in the drama Les deux gosses/The Two Boys (Fernand Rivers, 1936) based on the 1880 novel of the same name by Pierre Decourcelle, which had previously been made into a popular silent film. He also produced that year, the mystery Mister Flow/Compliments of Mister Flow (Robert Siodmak, 1936) starring Fernand Gravey, Edwige Feuillère, and Louis Jouvet. It was based on the 1927 novel Mister Flow by Gaston Leroux. Rivers then directed the comedy Berlingot et compagnie/Berlingot and Company (Fernand Rivers, 1939) starring Fernandel, Suzy Prim, and Fernand Charpin.

After the war, Fernand Rivers continued to direct films. His first post-war film was the romantic comedy Cyrano de Bergerac (Fernand Rivers, 1946) starring Claude Dauphin. After his own remake of Le Maître de forges/The Ironmaster (Fernand Rivers, 1948), he directed the comedy Ces dames aux chapeaux verts/The Ladies in the Green Hats (Fernand Rivers, 1949). His last film was Les Mains sales (Fernand Rivers, Simone Berriau, 1950) after Jean-Paul Sartre and starring Pierre Brasseur and Daniel Gélin. Rivers was also the distributor of Republic Picture films in France during the 1940s and 1950s. Fernand Rivers died in 1960 in Nice, France, at the age of 80.
